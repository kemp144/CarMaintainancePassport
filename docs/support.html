Frequently asked questions

Is my data private?

Yes. Everything you enter stays on your device by default. No account is required. The main exception is optional VIN autofill, which sends the VIN you enter to the NHTSA VIN decoder service only when you tap Autofill. See the Privacy Policy for full details.

Where are my backups stored?

If iCloud backup is available for your installed build and enabled on your device, backup snapshots are saved to your personal iCloud Drive under the "Car Maintenance Passport" folder. If iCloud is not available, backups are saved locally in the app's Documents folder. You can check the current storage location, create a fresh backup, and restore the latest backup in Settings → Backup.

Will my data survive if I delete and reinstall the app?

If iCloud backup is available, the app offers your latest backup for restore the first time you open it after reinstalling. If you skip that prompt, you can still restore the latest backup later from Settings → Backup.

How do I restore a purchase?

Open Settings inside the app and tap "Restore Purchases". Your Pro access is tied to your Apple ID and can be restored on any device signed in to the same account at no extra charge.

What is included in Pro?

Pro unlocks Ownership Intelligence analytics, all export options (PDF, Resale Report, CSV), the full Document Vault, and unlimited vehicles. Free users can track one vehicle with full service history and fuel logging.

Can I export my data?

Yes. With Pro, you can export a Service Passport PDF, a Resale Report PDF, or a CSV of your service history from the vehicle detail screen.

The app lost my data after an update. What happened?

Occasionally a major model update may require the local database to be reset. When this happens, the app shows a notice and moves the old data to a "Recovered Stores" folder inside Application Support. If you had iCloud backup enabled, your data can be restored from the backup offered on first launch.

How does the Ownership Forecast work?

The forecast estimates upcoming costs based on your active reminders and your service history. If a reminder has a due date within the next 12 months, its cost is estimated from the average of previous services of the same type. Items with no history use a conservative estimate until more records are logged.
